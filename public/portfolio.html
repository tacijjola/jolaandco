EDRI Central — European EV Network Intelligence Platform

Client: E.ON Drive Infrastructure  ·  Role: BI Lead, Passenger Cars Branch

The European EV market moves fast — new networks, new pricing strategies, new site openings, across 10 countries simultaneously. E.ON Drive had no centralised system to track any of it. EDRI Central was built to change that: a live, multi-branch intelligence platform covering both passenger car and commercial vehicle networks across EU markets. I led the passenger cars branch — scoping the intelligence framework, building the Databricks data foundation, and delivering the published Power BI reporting layer. A cross-functional team continues expanding the platform.

The Problem

  • No structured view of competitor EV networks across 10 EU markets
  • Passenger cars and trucks required separate frameworks and ownership
  • Raw data existed but was unprocessed — no repeatable intelligence methodology
  • No single platform to bring network growth, pricing, and expansion data together

What I Built

  • Databricks database consolidating all available EU competitor data — structured for ongoing ingestion
  • Data architecture designed to merge cleanly with future streams (weather data next)
  • Passenger cars intelligence framework: network monitoring, pricing, expansion tracking
  • Power BI report published and live for the cross-functional team

Outcomes & Impact

10
EU markets covered in one intelligence platform for the first time
Live
Power BI report published and active across the team
Future-proof
Architecture built for ongoing ingestion — weather data and more
Active
Cross-functional team continuing to build on the foundation

Approach

  • Scoped and led the passenger cars intelligence branch alongside a Senior Network Account Manager who owned the trucks branch
  • Built Databricks database: processed all available up-to-date EU competitor data, designed schema for clean future merges
  • Collaborated cross-functionally: Data Engineer, academic researcher (thesis), and Network Account Manager across two workstreams
  • Published Power BI report as the live reporting and intelligence layer for the team
  • Designed for continuity: clean architecture and documentation so the team can keep building after handover

Finance & Ops Dashboards + Board-Level Investment Analysis

Client: SWARCO Smart Charging  ·  Role: BI & Analytics Specialist

SWARCO's Finance and Operations leadership needed visibility they couldn't get from their existing reports — scattered across systems, too slow to generate, and not designed for decision-making. On top of that, strategic investment decisions on EV charging infrastructure required a rigorous analytical framework that could survive board-level scrutiny. I was brought in to build both: the operational reporting layer and the investment analysis that went to the board.

The Problem

  • Finance & Ops reporting fragmented across multiple source systems
  • No self-service BI — leadership dependent on manual data pulls
  • Investment decisions made without a structured analytical framework
  • Board needed a clear, defensible view of infrastructure ROI

What I Built

  • Power BI dashboards for Finance leadership — P&L, cost tracking, KPIs
  • Operations dashboard — utilisation, deployment status, performance trends
  • Investment analysis model evaluating EV infrastructure opportunities
  • Board-ready outputs: scenario modelling, risk view, recommendation layer

Outcomes & Impact

Self-service
Leadership could answer their own data questions — no analyst dependency
Board
Investment analysis presented at board level and approved
Finance + Ops
Two functions aligned on one reporting layer for the first time
Defensible
Scenario & risk modelling held up under board-level challenge

Approach

  • Stakeholder discovery: workshops with Finance and Ops leads to map reporting needs and decision workflows
  • Data architecture: connected disparate source systems into a clean Power BI data model
  • Dashboard design: built for decision-making — not just data display. KPIs surfaced at the top, drill-through for detail
  • Investment model: structured ROI framework with scenario sensitivity analysis and risk flags
  • Board delivery: translated analytical findings into clear executive narrative with recommendation layer
